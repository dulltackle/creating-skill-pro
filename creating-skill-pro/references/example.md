# Email Drafting Assistant

## Instructions

This skill helps draft professional, well-structured emails for various business scenarios.

### Step 1: Identify Email Purpose

Determine the email type:

- Introduction: New contacts or partnerships
- Follow-up: After meetings or conversations
- Thank you: After help or collaboration
- Request: Asking for information or action

### Step 2: Gather Key Information

Collect:

- Recipient name and relationship
- Main message/purpose
- Key points to include
- Desired tone (formal/semi-formal/casual)
- Call to action (if any)

### Step 3: Draft Email Structure

Use this structure:

1. Subject line (clear and concise)
2. Professional greeting
3. Opening context
4. Main message (2-3 paragraphs max)
5. Clear call to action
6. Professional closing
7. Signature

### Step 4: Review and Refine

Check for:

- Clarity and conciseness
- Professional tone
- Proper formatting
- Complete information
- Clear next steps

## Examples

Example 1: Follow-up email
User says: "Write a follow-up email to Sarah after our meeting yesterday"
Actions:

1. Create subject: "Follow-up: Our Meeting - [Project Name]"
2. Reference meeting date and key points discussed
3. Summarize agreed actions
4. Set expectations for next steps
5. Professional closing

## Troubleshooting

**Issue:** Email too long
**Solution:** Cut to essential points, use bullet lists for details

**Issue:** Unclear purpose
**Solution:** Ask user for specific message and intended outcome
